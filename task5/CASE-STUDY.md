## Phishing


Phishing is a type of cybercrime in which attackers trick individuals into sharing
sensitive information such as passwords, bank details, or OTPs. It usually takes place
through fake emails, SMS messages, or websites that appear to be from trusted sources
like banks, government agencies, or popular apps.
In a typical phishing attack, the victim receives a message that creates urgency or fear,
such as a warning about account suspension or an o@er that seems too good to be true.
The message contains a link that redirects the user to a fake website designed to look
identical to the original one. When the victim enters their login credentials or financial
details, the attacker captures this information and uses it for fraud or identity theft.
Phishing attacks target a wide range of people, including students, working
professionals, and elderly individuals. People who frequently use online banking, digital
payments, or social media are especially vulnerable. Those who are less aware of
cybersecurity practices are more likely to fall for such scams.
The consequences of phishing can be severe, including financial loss, data theft, and
misuse of personal information. Therefore, it is important to stay cautious, avoid
clicking on suspicious links, and always verify the authenticity of messages before
sharing any sensitive information.
